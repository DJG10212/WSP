Target Audience:

The site was designed for an agency or design company. The target audience would most likely be for other businesses, and business owners, so most likely people 25 years and up. Generally a company looking for a design agency will be an established business, or one with decent funding.


Client/User Needs:

The client needs people to easily navigate across the site, and the user needs to be able to read the navigation tabs. The user also needs the current page to be highlighted, so they have an easy point of reference as to what page they are currently on.


Critique:

The navbar design is very clean and modern. The sans serif font helps with the modern feel, while the all caps helps to relay their importance. The red block makes it very easy to know what page you are on, and helps create an awareness of your current page location. It is very bold, but still has a very metro modern feel. Red is also a great color for grabbing the users attention.

Target Audience:

This page is catering towards people seeking more information. It could essentially work for any age range, as it's a fairly generic contact form. Generally contact forms are used for two different groups of people. Those seeking more information becasue they are interested in the sites product/service, and people who are confused or need help.


Client/User Needs:

The client needs a well designed, and simple contact form, so they are able to answer customer questions, so that they do not lose sales. The user needs an easy to use contact form, that is simple enough, that they are willing to fill it out. The user also needs a phone number, email address, and location (when applicable).


Critique:

This contact form splits the page roughly in half. The color used is a turquoise blue, which is genrally used to convey clarity of thought, which can be useful in a contact form. There are only three fields to fill out, which is also welcoming to the user, as you can fill out the form in a matter of seconds. The right side of the page is a unique design. The background image is of a city, presumably the city in which the company is located. Over it, there is a turquoise transparent layer. All of the companies relevant contact information is displayed, with the useful information being larger, and displayed in white which contrasts well on the turquoise background.

Pricing Tables


Target Audience:

The target audience is any user interested in buying the product. They can be used for a wide variety of things, therefore the age and social class of the user can vary greatly.


Client/User Needs:

Clients will use pricing tables to relay their exact prices to the user. They will try to have as enticing of information as they can, in order to get the user to buy their product. The users will use pricing tables to easily compare the different levels of the product. They are great for comparing information, and making a final decision.


Critique:

This is a great example of a simple, yet efficient pricing table. The most commonly used, or best deal, is highlighted to entice users. The red numbers and buttons are great for attracting attention. The titles are bold and in all caps, to show their importance, but not enough to overpower the prices. The features are all displayed inline with each other, to make it easy for comparing between each table. Less relevant information is displayed outside of the pricing tables, to show that they are less important. The most important part, the "Sign Up" buttons are displayed large, in order to entice users to click them.



Footer


Target Audience:

Generally for people who are confused, or looking for contact info and social media sites. Applies to all ages, and any demographics.


Client/User Needs:

Clients need a footer, as they often have very important information. The users use footers to find anything from contact info, social media sites, and general "About Us" information. There are often links to the main pages, which can be helpful for a confused user.


Critique:

Grey is often a neutral color, and is great for a footer, as it doesn't stand out. The majority of the font and icons on the footer are a grey, in order to not draw attention, unless you are specifically seeking the information. The footer is broken into three segments, with relevant information in each. The titles stand out ever so slightly, because they are larger, and a white color. The only real "exciting" part of the footer is the top orange stripe which makes it more intriguing, while still profossionally relaying the proper information below.
